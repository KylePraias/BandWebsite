Band Members

Deryck Whibley

Vocals, Guitar (1997 - 2025)

As the founder and only constant member of Sum 41, Deryck has led the band as the lead vocalist and rhythm guitarist since its inception.

Dave Baksh

Guitar (1997 - 2006, 2015 - 2025)

Joining in 1997 as the lead guitarist and backing vocalist, Dave departed in 2006 to pursue other projects but returned to the band in 2015.

Jason McCaslin

Bass Guitar (1999 - 2025)

Jason became the band's bassist and backing vocalist in 1999, contributing to Sum 41's rhythm section ever since.

Tom Thacker

Guitar (2009 - 2025)

Initially joining as a touring member in 2007, Tom became an official member in 2009, serving as co-lead guitarist, keyboardist, and backing vocalist.

Frank Zummo

Drums (2015 - 2025)

Frank took over drumming duties in 2015, bringing his percussive talents to the band's dynamic sound.

Steve Jocz

Drums (1996 - 2013)

A co-founder of Sum 41, Steve was the band's original drummer and backing vocalist from 1996 until his departure in 2013.

Jon Marshall

Guitar (1996 - 1997)

Serving as the lead vocalist and rhythm guitarist in the band's early days from 1996 to 1997, Jon was part of the original lineup.

Richard Roy

Bass Guitar (1996 - 1998)

Richard handled bass duties and backing vocals from 1996 until 1998, contributing to the band's early development.

History

Sum 41, formed in 1996 in Ajax, Ontario, Canada, quickly rose to prominence with their distinctive fusion of punk rock and heavy metal. The original lineup featured lead vocalist and guitarist Deryck Whibley, drummer Steve Jocz, guitarist Dave Baksh, and bassist Jason McCaslin. Their energetic performances and genre-blending sound garnered a dedicated fanbase in the early 2000s. The band's debut album, All Killer No Filler (2001), achieved international success, propelled by the hit single "Fat Lip," which topped the Billboard Modern Rock Tracks chart. This success was followed by albums like Does This Look Infected? (2002) and Chuck (2004), showcasing their ability to blend catchy melodies with heavier riffs. Over the years, Sum 41 continued to evolve, experimenting with different styles while maintaining their core punk ethos. Throughout their career, Sum 41 faced various challenges, including lineup changes and health issues. Despite these obstacles, they released several more albums, such as Underclass Hero (2007), Screaming Bloody Murder (2011), and 13 Voices (2016), each reflecting their growth as musicians and individuals. Their relentless touring schedule and dynamic live shows solidified their reputation as a formidable force in the rock scene. In 2024, Sum 41 announced that their eighth studio album, Heaven :x: Hell, would be their final release, marking the end of an era for the band and their fans. The double album encapsulates the duality of their musical journey, combining elements of their signature punk sound with heavier metal influences.
